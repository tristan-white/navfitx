# Validation

A performance evaluation report is "valid" if it adheres to all the rules outlines in [BUPERSINST 1610.10H](https://www.mynavyhr.navy.mil/Portals/55/Reference/Instructions/BUPERS/BUPERSINST%201610.10.pdf?ver=g42WV7fkucvkkZolLrWseA%3d%3d).

BUPERSINT 1610.10H is a massive document; the most recent version is 171 pages. Due to the number and complexity of validation requirements, many invalid reports are submitted to NAVPERSCOM every year. NAVFIT98 has tried to reduce this number, but it has still failed to catch many validation issues. Approximately 10% of reports submitted each year are invalid.[^1]

NAVFITX improves upon NAVFIT98 by adding additional validation checks. When you export a NAVFITX report as a PDF, it will warn you if the report is invalid and tell you why it has failed validation.

## Why It Matters

Performance evaluation reports are the basis for promotions. Thus, it's important that these reports are created correctly, and that they capture data that accurately reflects a sailor's performance.

NAVFIT98 fails to comprehensively validate reports, resulting in thousands of reports being rejected each year, and many invalid reports being uploaded into sailors' official records.

[^1]: [Performance Evaluation FAQ](https://www.mynavyhr.navy.mil/Career-Management/Performance-Evaluation/FAQ/)
